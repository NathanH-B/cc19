2. As so-called “digital natives”, where do you see literacy (of language, image, culture, tech, etc.) valued and/or devalued on the Web or amongst users of the Web? Are there notable advantages or disadvantages of any perceived shifts in recent years?

I think there has been a massive shift towards video formats for information. It used to be if you wanted to learn how to do something you would read an article about it. Know you watch a youtube tutorial. Us digital natives hate long articles. And really anything that takes a lot of time. We have developed shorter attention spans. Or more like we demand things to be faster. We like to get our information in short bursts. Instead of buying a book on how to fly a kite. We watch a 5 minute video. I think in some ways wanting information faster and more quickly is a gond thing. Many things can be explained much faster in fewer words. This puts a lot of pressure on content creators though. Arguability writing a short article on how to fly a kite is quicker there making a video. To write something you can do it anywhere at anytime and all you have to do is write it down. For a video, you have to go out film, make sure the lighting is right, Take multiple takes. Have a script, and edit. It takes so much longer to make a video but at the end it's easier to understand.

3. How do you see the expanding or limiting nature of hypertext on today's Web? Do Delany's criticisms of hypertext still hold true within the modern Web and all of its growth and advancements

I agree with Denalys opinion that all texts are hypertexts. That if you see something you don't understand a word you can look it up in the dictionary. All that hypertext does is do the work for you. However this is concerning because hypertext can be used in an academic or news setting to control the narrative. For example if you are reading an article about astronomy and there is a piece of hypertext about the earth that links you to a flat earth conspiracy article someone who is just starting to learn about the planets could get misinformed...or exposed to the truth depending on who you ask. Jokes aside hypertext can be helpful and used in creative ways especially in nonlinear novels but people need to keep in mind that hypertext links can be often biased and purposely there to prove the authors point.
